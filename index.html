    System Design

    Why the environment feels advanced

    Cascading uncertainty

    Incidents appear mid-episode, which forces the policy to reserve capacity and value pre-positioning instead of greedily consuming every responder on turn one.

    Fairness-aware pressure

    High-vulnerability districts penalize neglect, so the reward is aligned with equitable public-service behavior instead of raw throughput alone.

    Operational realism

    Travel time, hospital overflow, mutual aid costs, and resource specialization make the action space feel like command-and-control, not ticket routing.

    Hackathon-ready judging

    The environment outputs both step rewards and a judge-friendly summary across outcome, timeliness, fairness, efficiency, and resilience.
